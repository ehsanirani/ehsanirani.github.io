About

I'm a computational physicist and data scientist.

I completed my BSc and MSc in physics at the University of Tehran and Sharif University of Technology in Iran. Later, I pursued a PhD at Georg-August-Universität Göttingen, where my research delved into the field of soft matter physics, titled "Rheology of Soft Attractive Particles."

Subsequently, during my post-doctoral research at the Max Delbrück Center for Molecular Medicine in Berlin, I explored physical mechanisms involved in 3D genome organization.

Currently, I work as a senior data scientist. In my free time, I enjoy contributing to open-source and scientific projects, with a primary focus on active matter research. Recently, I've developed a strong passion for coding in Julia. For more insights into my work, please visit the Projects section and the Publications page.

Beyond my professional endeavors, I maintain a keen interest in computers, science, education, and other topics that impact human life, such as politics.
